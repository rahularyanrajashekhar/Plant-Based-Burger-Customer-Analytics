# Plant-Based Burger Customer Analytics Strategy

## Project Overview
This project analyses customer preferences for a proposed plant-based burger launch for McDonald’s Australia. The objective was to use customer analytics to understand consumer preferences, identify target segments, and recommend a product and marketing strategy that reduces launch risk and improves market fit.

## Business Problem
McDonald’s Australia was considered for entry into the growing plant-based burger market. The project aimed to understand what product attributes customers prefer and how different customer segments respond to price, patty type, bread type, and product customisation.

## Tools Used
- Qualtrics – survey design and data collection
- Tableau – cluster analysis and visualisation
- Excel – data preparation and analysis
- Conjoint Analysis – customer preference analysis
- STP Framework – segmentation, targeting, and positioning

## Survey Design
The survey collected customer responses on burger preferences, purchase behaviour, spending habits, and demographic information. It also included conjoint-style product profiles to evaluate customer preference across different burger attributes.

## Key Attributes Analysed
- Patty Type: Paneer, Potato, Cauliflower, Soy
- Bread Type: Multigrain, Brioche, Whole Wheat, Gluten Free
- Number of Patties: Single, Double
- Price: $8.95 and $11.95

## Methodology
1. Designed and distributed a customer preference survey using Qualtrics.
2. Collected survey responses and analysed customer behaviour patterns.
3. Conducted conjoint analysis to identify preferred product attributes.
4. Used Tableau K-Means clustering to segment customers based on part-worth preference scores.
5. Developed customer segment profiles and product positioning recommendations.
6. Created a digital marketing plan using SEO, SEM, and social media campaign strategies.

## Key Insights
- Patty type was the most important attribute in customer preference.
- Paneer and Potato were the most preferred patty options.
- Multigrain bread was preferred as a healthier bread option.
- Customers showed interest in a mid-priced plant-based burger around $11.95.
- Two customer segments were identified:
  - Value-Focused Millennials
  - Experimental Urban Adults

## Recommendations
- Launch a value-focused plant-based burger using familiar ingredients such as Paneer or Potato.
- Offer customisation options including healthier bread choices.
- Position the product as a balance between affordability, taste, and health-conscious indulgence.
- Use digital marketing campaigns, app-based promotions, influencer marketing, and SEO/SEM strategies to target younger consumers.
